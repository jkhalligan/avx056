How IT Works

How an inheritance advance works.

An inheritance advance (also a called probate advance) is an advance of money to a beneficiary through reassignment of an inheritance. You reassign all or a portion of your inheritance and you are sent a lump sum of the money even before probate is complete. An inheritance advance does not have to be repaid. It is not a loan.

First our experts evaluate the details of your inheritance and offer you an advance. If agreed, we send you the money wait for the estate to close and are then compensated directly from the estate based on our mutual agreement. We take the risk while you benefit by receiving your inheritance funds without the delay.
